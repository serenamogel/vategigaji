Mikaela Shiffrin | Career

Her first victory came in the slalom at Trofeo Topolino in 2010, making her the first American girl to win a junior race in Italy.

In 2011, Shiffrin won a bronze medal at the Junior World Championships, and in March 2011 she made her World Cup debut, winning the slalom event at the US Championships.

Shiffrin won her maiden World Cup slalom in 2012, and she went on to win three more World Cup slaloms and a gold medal at the 2013 World Championships.

Later, in 2014, she competed in the Winter Olympics in Sochi and won the gold medal for America in the women’s slalom, making her the youngest participant to win the gold medal in an Olympics and the first American to win the event in over 40 years.

Mikaela Shiffrin has won almost 40 World Cup races in total, which is the fifth most by a female alpine skier.
