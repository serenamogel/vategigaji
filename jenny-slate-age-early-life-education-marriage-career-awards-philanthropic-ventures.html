Jenny Slate: Discover the multifaceted talent of Jenny Slate.

Jenny Slate is an American actress, comedian, and author who was born on March 25, 1982, in Massachusetts.

She is best known for her voice performances in animated films and TV shows.

Some of them inlcude Zootopia, The Secret Life of Pets, and Big Mouth, as well as her leading role in the comedy-drama film Obvious Child, for which she won several awards.

Additionally, she is also the co-creator, writer, and producer of the children’s short film and book series Marcel the Shell with Shoes On, which became a viral sensation.

She has also appeared in other films and TV shows, such as Gifted, Everything Everywhere All At Once, Parks and Recreation, and Saturday Night Live.

Her Early Life, Family, Siblings And Education

Jenny Slate, born on March 25, 1982, in Massachusetts, hails from a Jewish family.

Her parents are Ron Slate, a businessman and poet, and Nancy Slate, a housewife and potter.

She has two sisters, Abigail and Stacey.

Additionally, Jenny’s cultural background includes a grandmother born in Cuba and raised in France.

Growing up in Milton, Massachusetts, she attended Milton Academy, where she graduated as valedictorian.

Subsequently, she pursued literature at Columbia University, engaging in the improv and comedy scene before graduating in 2004.

Her Personal Life Marriage, Divorce And Relaionship Status

In 2012, Jenny Slate married filmmaker and editor Dean Fleischer-Camp, whom she had met in 2009.

The couple collaborated on various projects, including Marcel the Shell with Shoes On.

However, they divorced in 2016 due to irreconcilable differences.

Despite having no children, Jenny has had relationships with celebrities like actor Chris Evans and art curator Ben Shattuck, though none of these relationships endured.

Slate’s Professional Career And Breakthrough

Beginning her career as a comedian in New York City, Jenny Slate performed stand-up and improv, forming the duo Gabe & Jenny with Gabe Liedman.

She co-hosted the weekly show Big Terrific with Max Silvestri.

Her television appearances included shows like Late Night with Jimmy Fallon, Bored to Death, and Girls.

She entered the film industry with her debut in Alvin and the Chipmunks: Chipwrecked in 2011.

Jenny gained prominence on Saturday Night Live’s 35th season in 2009 but faced dismissal after unintentionally using the F-word on air.

Subsequently, she focused on voice acting, contributing to animated films and TV shows such as The Lorax, Zootopia, and Big Mouth.

She created, wrote, and produced the widely popular children’s short film and book series Marcel the Shell with Shoes On.

Her live-action roles included films like Obvious Child, Gifted, and Everything Everywhere All At Once, along with TV shows such as Parks and Recreation and House of Lies.

Jenny Slate earned critical acclaim for her performance in Obvious Child, a comedy-drama exploring a stand-up comedian’s journey through an unexpected pregnancy and abortion.

She also wrote and starred in her Netflix comedy special, Jenny Slate: Stage Fright, in 2019.

Jenny’s Awards And Achievements

Jenny Slate’s contributions have earned her various awards and nominations:

  • Critics’ Choice Movie Award for Best Actress in a Comedy for Obvious Child in 2015.
  • Women Film Critics Circle Award for Best Comedic Actress for Obvious Child in 2014.
  • Gotham Independent Film Award for Breakthrough Actor for Obvious Child in 2014.
  • Behind the Voice Actors Award for Best Vocal Ensemble in a Feature Film for Zootopia in 2017.
  • New York Times Best Seller for Marcel the Shell with Shoes On: Things About Me in 2011.
