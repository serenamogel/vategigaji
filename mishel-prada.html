Mishel Prada is an American actress, writer, and producer who is known for having been cast as Emma Hernandez in the TV drama series, Vida, as well as for playing the role of Gabi in the web series, Fear the Walking Dead: Passage. Over the years, she has also been a part of a number of other shows and films which include the likes of Eat Spirit Eat, There Is a New World Somewhere, Benjamin Troubles, A Handful of Dust, Bachelor Lions, The Two Dogs, Corporate, Wendy: The Wendy Williams Show, Last Call with Carson Daly, and Sidewalks Entertainment. Moreover, Mishel Prada has also tried her hand at producing and writing by working on a couple of such projects. She has also garnered a decent social media fan base with more than 50k followers on Instagram and more than 8k followers on Twitter.

First Film

Mishel Prada made her first feature film appearance by playing the role of Ana in the comedy-drama film, Eat Spirit Eat, in 2013. The film was premiered at the First Glance Film Festival on September 20, 2013, and the cast also included actors Owen Williams, Adriana Mather, Ezequiel Stremiz, Clayton Rohner, Kristin Minter, and Leah Briese.

In 2016, Mishel Prada made her first theatrical film appearance by playing the role of Angela in the romantic adventure drama film, There Is a New World Somewhere, which was premiered at the Los Angeles Asian Pacific Film Festival on April 26, 2015. The film included actors such as Agnes Bruckner, Maurice Compte, Ashley Bell, John Robinson, Raymond Vrazel Jr., Morgan Krantz, and Henry Dittman.

First Web Show

Mishel Prada made her first web show appearance by playing the role of Gabi in an episode of the post-apocalyptic horror drama series, Fear the Walking Dead: Passage, in October 2016.

First TV Show

Mishel Prada made her first TV show appearance by playing the role of Trudy the Trustafarian in an episode, titled Powerpoint of Death, of the dark comedy series, Corporate, in January 2018. Directed by Pat Bishop, the lead cast of the show comprised of actors Matt Ingebretson, Jake Weisman, Anne Dudek, Adam Lustick, Aparna Nancherla, and Lance Reddick.
