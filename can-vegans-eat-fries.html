Are fried fries vegan?

In simple terms, French fries are thinly sliced potato strips which are deep fried, and then dusted with a liberal sprinkling of salt, and sometimes other seasonings as well. So, on paper, French fries are vegan as they're basically thin strips of potato fried in oil and then dusted with salt.

Why are fries not vegan?

Unfortunately, McDonald's fries are not vegetarian in the United States as the fries use milk and beef flavoring. Previously, McDonald's fries did use animal lard during cooking, but the company switched to using vegetable oil, which made the fries lose their flavor.

What can't vegans eat?

Vegans can't eat any foods made from animals, including:

  • Beef, pork, lamb, and other red meat.
  • Chicken, duck, and other poultry.
  • Fish or shellfish such as crabs, clams, and mussels.
  • Eggs.
  • Cheese, butter.
  • Milk, cream, ice cream, and other dairy products.
  • Mayonnaise (because it includes egg yolks)
  • Honey.
